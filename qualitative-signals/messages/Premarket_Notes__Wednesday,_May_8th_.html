Signs of Disinflation/Recession/Stagflation? Yesterday, the first quarter Federal Reserve’s Senior Loan Officer Opinion Survey (SLOOS), which provides quarterly information on loan demand and bank lending tightness, was released. The SLOOS found that more US banks reported stricter credit standards in the first quarter and declining loan demand. The loan demand declined across all major segments, including commercial real estate, small businesses, and consumer loans (autos, homes, etc.).

 

With the outlook for inflation undetermined, i.e., the Fed “remains data dependent,” next week’s CPI number is likely to move the market in one direction or the other. We note that countries around the globe are either lowering rates or talking about lowering rates in the face of declining economic activity (global recession watch).
