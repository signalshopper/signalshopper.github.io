US stocks look to open lower - with banking shares taking the hardest hits - after China announced it would impose a 34% tariff on all US goods starting Apr 10 in retaliation for the US’s tariffs.

 

Valuation: Many investors are blaming tariffs for the performance of the decline of their 401Ks today, but we want to remind investors that the recent market action is not unprecedented. The S&P 500 futures (SPX) rose 76% from the Oct ’22 bottom of 3492 to the Feb ‘25 top of 6147. Since then, the SPX has fallen 751 points or ~12%. Valuation aside, markets will look to Fed Chairman Powell’s 11:25 ET comments for reassurance.

 

From a technical standpoint, today is an important day for the market. The S&P 500 futures (SPX) needs to close above its 5400 support level. If the SPX cannot hold 5400, the following support level is 5,265.
