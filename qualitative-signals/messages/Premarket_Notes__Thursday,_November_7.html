Markets

 

Premarket Futures:  DJIA +0.01%  S&P +0.30%  NASDAQ +0.55%  10YR -3 bps  Gold +0.57%  Oil -0.60%  BTC +1.0%

 

US stocks ripped yesterday (S&P +2%, NASDAQ  +1.9%and the Dow +1240 points) after DJ Trump’s decisive election win raised expectations favorable tax cuts, deregulation, and tariffs will boost economic growth and corporate profits. Trump’s win is positive for financials, industrials, commodities, and energy but unfavorable for real estate (higher rates), utilities (less competitive yields), and consumer staples.

 

Today, a falling 10YR yield “could” be perceived as negative ahead of today’s FOMC meeting (25-50 bps) and Fed Chairman Powell’s 2:30 ET “data dependent” comments. The argument for a 50 bps rate cut is that higher rates dampen consumer demand. We remind investors that rates are rising due to the Treasury’s need to fund deficits, not because the economy is constantly expanding.
