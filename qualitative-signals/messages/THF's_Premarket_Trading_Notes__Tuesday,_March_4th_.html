Yesterday, the indexes continued their correction with the DOW -10%, the NASDAQ 2.6%, and the SPX -1.75%, and Wall Street pundits who have been breathlessly mansplaining why the markets can continue to rise (remember the Alamo Y2K bubble) are now blaming their irrational exuberance on tariffs (25% on Canada and Mexico, 10% on China). Year-to-date, the SPX is -0.54%, and the AI-heavy NASDAQ is -4.97%. Year-over-year, the SPX is +14%, and the NASDAQ is +13.2%. A straw man might note that diversification (SPY) provides better returns than a more speculative (NASDAQ).

 

The latest ISM report showed that US manufacturing slowed more than expected in February, driven by weaker demand, slower production, and rising prices. Investors will now focus on Friday’s monthly jobs report and tonight’s Presidential address to Congress.

 

Tariffs be damned, yesterday, US Manufacturing PMI rose to 52.7 in February versus expectations of 51.6, improving from January’s 51.2. The reading signaled the sector’s 2nd consecutive month of expansion and the strongest growth since June 2022. Output grew at the strongest pace since May 2022, while new orders rose the fastest in a year.

 

Crypto Reserve Blowback: Including multiple cryptocurrencies in a US strategic reserve has raised concerns. Critics argue that adding altcoins to a Bitcoin reserve complicates the initiative and could create unintended market biases. We find it hard to disagree. BTC, ETH, and SOL make strategic sense to us; XRP and ADA not so much. What follows is a brief description of the digital coins mentioned.

  • * Bitcoin (BTC): A decentralized currency (a digital store of value) that disintermediates banks and administrators (economic grifters), transacting electronically and via a peer-to-peer network. Market Cap: $1.8T
  • * Ethereum (ETH): An open-source blockchain platform that enables the creation of smart contracts and decentralized applications (DApps). This allows for various use cases beyond digital currency, such as supply chain management, voting systems, legal contracts, etc. Market Cap: $275B
  • * Solana (SOL): A high-performance blockchain platform that utilizes a unique consensus algorithm called “Proof of History” to achieve fast transaction speeds and low fees (great for meme coin production). Market Cap: $80B
  • * XRP: a digital asset native to the XRP Ledger. Anyone with a cryptographic key and an internet connection can receive, hold, and send XRP to anyone else. XRP’s creators have developed it as a desirable bridge currency that can facilitate trades in any other currency. Ripple is the payment protocol and exchange network using XRP, the digital asset native to the XRP Ledger. Market Cap: $150B
  • * Cardano (ADA): A decentralized public blockchain and cryptocurrency project that is fully open source. Cardano is developing a smart contract platform that seeks to deliver more advanced features than any protocol previously developed. Market cap: $34B
