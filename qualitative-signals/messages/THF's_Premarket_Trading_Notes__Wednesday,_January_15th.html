Premarket Futures

 

It must be Groundhog Day. The annual inflation rate rose for the third consecutive month to 2.9% in December from 2.7% in November, but in line with expectations of 2.9%. The annual Core inflation, excluding food and energy, declined to 3.2% from 3.3%, and the monthly inflation showed a similar decline to 0.2% (inline) from 0.3% in the previous month. However, the monthly rate rose to 0.4% from 0.3%. These numbers will be subject to BLS revisions next month, but until then, the “steady as she goes” results will give rate-cut bulls a reason to cheer. On a positive note, Treasury yields and the USD will fall (positive for high-risk assets). We expect profit-taking at the open after the big premarket rally and then a grind upward.

 

BlackRock, Wells Fargo, and JPMorgan beat expectations. We await guidance.

 

Valuation: Monday, Fed Gov. Lisa Cook said stock markets (and corporate bonds) are “susceptible to large declines” at today’s prices. Lisa’s comments come on the heels of Goldman Sachs equity strategist Peter Oppenheimer, who said stocks are priced to perfection. “While we expect equity markets to make further progress over the year as a whole – largely driven by earnings – they are increasingly vulnerable to a correction driven either by further rises in bond yields and/or disappointments on growth in economic data or earnings.” We note that the market hasn’t made a higher high since early December.
