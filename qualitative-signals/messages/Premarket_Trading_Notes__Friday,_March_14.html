Are we there yet? Since 1928, the median S&P 500 correction is -13.6% and lasts about four months. If the current market (-9.7%) follows the trend, we can expect the correction to fall another 3.9% and end in mid-May. However, more recent history shows that, since 2008, stocks have typically declined for one month after the S&P 500 entered correction territory, with the index printing an average negative return of 1.7%, but rebounded an average of 2.1% over the following three months and ~5% over six months (Dow Jones Market Data).
