Markets

 

Markets look to continue their declines after yesterday’s comments from Federal Reserve Chairman Powell, which led traders to lower expectations for a rate cut in December. Refusing to comment on the policies of the new Administration, Powell said the economy is on track, and there’s no rush to lower rates. We believe investors took this as a hedge against pro-inflationary risks in the new Presidential term.

   

China: The world’s second-largest economy reported that its Industrial Production rose 5.3% year/year in October, weaker than expectations of 5.5% and slower than September’s 5.4% increase. China’s property investment also fell at a faster pace from January to October, declining 10.3% in the first 10 months of  ‘24 from a year earlier. (global recession watch?)
