Markets

 

Political and Trade Turmoil: Markets slumped over the weekend as investors digested President Trump’s call for Fed Chairman Powell’s head and concerns US trade negotiations could turn into a trade war after China threatened to retaliate against “any countries that work with the US to isolate the Chinese economy.”

 

We also note that European equity funds are reportedly pulling funds out of US equities (est’d $10.62 B YTD). The removal of European demand suggests that when the tariff issue is resolved, the markets will likely rip higher without notice. Stay nimble.

 

Talking Heads and Earnings: Beware, beginning tomorrow, nine Federal Reserve Speakers will share their economic wisdom this week, concluding with Friday’s market-moving Consumer Sentiment report for April. Major earnings reports begin Tuesday and include Tesla (TSLA), SAP (SAP), Capital One (COF), Alphabet (GOOG), Boeing (BA), Lockheed Martin (LMT), Intel (INTC), et al., as investors look for clues on how the tariff chatter affects the technology, banking and defense companies.
