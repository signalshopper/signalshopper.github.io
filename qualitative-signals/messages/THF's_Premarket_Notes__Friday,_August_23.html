Are we in a recession?

A new Sahm-like recession indicator combines vacancy with unemployment data. The indicator factors the minimum of the Sahm indicator (the difference between the 3-month trailing average of the unemployment rate and its minimum over the past 12 months) and adds the vacancy rate. On average, the new indicator detects recessions 1.4 months after they have started, compared to the Sahm rule, which detects a recession 2.6 months after it starts. The new indicator also has a better historical track record. It perfectly identifies all recessions since 1930, while the Sahm rule breaks down before 1960.

 

When the new Sahn-like indicator reaches 0.3pp, a recession may have started; when the indicator reaches 0.8pp, a recession has started. With July 2024 data, the indicator is at 0.5pp, so the probability that the US economy is now in recession is 40% and may have started as early as March. https://pascalmichaillat.org/16.pdf (Source: Macro Strategy Partners)
