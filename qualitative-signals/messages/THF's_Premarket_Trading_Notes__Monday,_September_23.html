Today, and for the next few days, we will hear a parade of Fed speakers provide us with their mea culpa post-FOMC meeting commentary. We hope they are informative after signaling the wheels are falling off the economic bus last week.

 

On Thursday, the final Q2 GDP number will be released (3.0% expected), and then on Friday, August consumption (+0.3%, down from +0.5%) and core PCE inflation (+0.18%, up from +0.16%) will be revealed. Stronger economic data and loose (inflating) monetary policy should be bullish for stocks, especially mid and small-caps (IWM).
