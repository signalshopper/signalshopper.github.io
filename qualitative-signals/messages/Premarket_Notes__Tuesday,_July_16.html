Markets

 

Premarket Futures:  DOW +0.41%  S&P +0.23%  NASDAQ +0.28%  Gold +0.44% 10YR -2 bps  Crude -1.53%

 

Markets look to open cautiously higher after yesterday’s dovish comments from Fed Chairman Powell and this morning’s weak June retail sales numbers, indicating consumer spending may be slowing. Treasury yields declined on the news (10YR -2 bps). Crude prices fell after China missed its Q2 GDP expectations (4.7% versus 5.1%). Digital currencies continue to benefit from “Trump Trade” enthusiasm.

 

Recession Watch: Salesforce reportedly terminated 300 jobs this month, following several other large tech companies who similarly fired large numbers of workers this month. Intuit announced plans to eliminate 1,800 employees last week, while software makers UiPath and Open Text also announced firings. (Bloomberg)
