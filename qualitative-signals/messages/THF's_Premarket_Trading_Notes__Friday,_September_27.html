Meh, the markets seem undecided to slightly positive ahead of today’s opening. On the one hand, August PCE declined 0.01% from July, but the Year/Year Core PCE was unchanged at 2.70%. Bulls will view the results as a signal that the Federal Reserve can continue aggressively cutting the Fed Funds rate. Bears will note the inability to arrest inflation and the negative but widely ignored recession signals. Wednesday, we provided valuation metrics. Today, we look at a few more classic signals.

 

Economic data continues to paint a picture of a weakening economy despite the Herculean attempts of the MSM to prevent voters from noticing before November 5th. In August, the forward-looking Conference Board’s Leading Economic Index (LEI) fell to 100.2, its lowest since October 2016, marking the 6th consecutive monthly decline. The ratio between leading and lagging indicators is currently under 0.85, the lowest since the 1950s, a recession signal. (WisdomTree)

 

 

Gold is a safe haven asset and an inflation hedge, while oil demand represents global demand and economic activity. Therefore, gold’s outperformance relative to oil often represents a sign of economic slowdown. The ratio between the price of gold futures and Brent crude futures is up over 35% this year, the highest since 2020. (CoinDesk)

 

 

Did the economy actually grow by 3.0% in the 2Q? The final estimate for Q2 GDP showed the economy expanded at a 3% annualized pace, unchanged from the prior estimate. However, consumer spending was revised down to 2.8% from 2.9%, while government spending rose 3.1% vs. the previously reported 2.7%. Government spending isn’t necessarily a judicious use of taxpayer money (see the Inflation Reduction Act, CHIPs Act, Defense Department, etc.) or a valuable economic growth metric. Government spending is often inflationary, nor does it create many private-sector jobs. In August, unemployment rose in 57% of the 50 states, Washington, DC, and Puerto Rico (MacroMicro). We can talk about the potential negative economic impact of the pending East Coast Port Strike, but why bother?
