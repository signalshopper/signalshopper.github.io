Recall that yesterday, the FOMC minutes emphasized that inflation must decline further before rate cuts.

 

“Bull markets are born on pessimism, grow on skepticism, mature on optimism, and die on euphoria.” ( Sir John Templeton) Put another way, the best time to buy stocks is when bearishness is high and bullishness is low.

 

According to the American Association of Individual Investors (AAII), individual investors are at their most bearish since November 2023, with 47.3% of respondents saying they are pessimistic about stocks over the next six months and just 28.4% bullish. The historical average is 37.5% bullish and 31.0% bearish. A month ago, 43.4% were bullish, and 29.4% were bearish, i.e., pessimism has increased. The AAII has surveyed investors weekly for more than 35 years, and the lesson from all those surveys is that investor sentiment is typically a contrarian indicator. (Source: Breitbart Business Digest)
