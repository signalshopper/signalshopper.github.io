Markets

 

This week looks to be reasonably quiet. Earnings season is winding down, and excluding Thursday's Initial Jobless Claims and Friday's preliminary Consumer Sentiment releases, the economic noise/news is light. We note the NASDAQ and the S&P 500 look to open higher but, as of now, remain below their 50-day moving averages.    

 

Treasury yields and the USD are lower after weaker-than-expected labor reports last week (positive risk assets and non-interest-bearing assets, i.e., Gold, Crypto). Energy prices are higher on talk of increasing tensions in the Middle East.  

 

I found this comment interesting, but requiring more analysis: "The yield curve was inverted for more than 500 days since 1920 (1929, 1974, 2008). The market eventually declined more than 50% in all three cases." Is this a warning sign?
