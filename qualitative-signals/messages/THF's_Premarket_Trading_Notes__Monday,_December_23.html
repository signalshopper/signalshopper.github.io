“Don’t worry, be happy.” Last Wednesday, the Federal Reserve performed as expected when it announced a 25 basis point Xmas rate reduction. However, the Fed spoiled the trading day when it said the three expected rate reductions in 2025 are, you guessed it, data-dependent. Then, on Thursday, the Administration’s lap dog, also known as the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS), reported that the personal-consumption-expenditures price index (PCE), the Fed’s favored measure of inflation, rose (inflationary), but it rose less than expected. Astute readers will note that the inflation and unemployment numbers have been manipulated monthly for at least the last 12 months.

 

Our conclusion: Year-end bonus-dependent money managers are on their knees, begging for the usual, but not always (see 2023), Santa Claus rally, which occurs between the last trading five days of December and the first two days of the New Year. We are cautious with the DOW and the Russell trading below their 50-day moving averages, but that said, “Don’t worry, be happy.” Year-to-date, the NASDAQ is up 30.4%, the S&P 500 is +24.3%, and the DOW is +13.7%.  

 

Those of us who are skeptical of people who make money with other people’s money (OPM) will note that Bitcoin (BTC), the people’s asset, is up 150.4% year-to-date as limited supply, growing government acceptance, and retail interest (ETFs) continue to drive demand.
