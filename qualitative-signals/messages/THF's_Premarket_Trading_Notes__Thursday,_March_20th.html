The indexes look to open lower after yesterday’s FOMC euphoria. The pundits blame the contraction on the “risks of economic contraction amid the US government’s disruptive [tariff] policies, but profit taking seems the more likely suspect.

 

FOMC thoughts from the cheap seats: “Recent indicators suggest that economic activity has continued to expand at a solid pace. The unemployment rate has stabilized at a low level in recent months, labor market conditions remain solid, and inflation remains somewhat elevated.” Therefore, there is no change in the fed funds rate from 4.25 % - 5.0%. Updated dot plot expectations show the committee expects two more 25 bps rate cuts by year-end.  

 

Treasury yields declined after the Federal Reserve announced it would reduce the pace of quantitative tightening (allowing bonds to mature without replacing them) by slowing the pace of its balance sheet tightening next month from $20B to $5B/month “amid signs of lower liquidity risks and a more conservative Treasury” (i.e., the debt ceiling).

 

We note that the Fed’s balance sheet was ~$4.25 trillion in 2020, ballooning to ~$9.0T in 2022 (+$4.75T of additional liquidity), and remains an elevated $6.75T today. A Straw Man might believe there’s still too much (inflation-causing, asset inflating) liquidity in the market.
