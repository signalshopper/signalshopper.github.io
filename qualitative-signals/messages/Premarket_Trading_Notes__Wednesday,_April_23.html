The 10YR treasury yield is down 10 basis points (bps) as US treasuries are once again in demand globally, stocks rose overnight and continue to climb this morning, and the USD is rising. What changed? Not much, but the markets appreciate a less bellicose US President.

 

Yesterday, markets rallied, erasing some of the sell-off driven by concerns about the Fed’s independence after Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent said he anticipates de-escalation in the US-China tariff standoff and news that Trump said he has “no intention” of firing Fed Chair J. Powell before his term ends. As a reminder, Powell does not make policy decisions on his own. The policy is set by the Federal Open Market Committee’s (FOMC) 12 voting members.

 

Is the Yen Carry Trade Pressuring US Markets? We’ve discussed the carry trade in the past, but we think it bears repeating. The carry trade is an arbitrage created by borrowing money in a market with low returns (Japan) and investing it in a market with higher returns (USA). Last June, the spread between the USD and the JPY had widened. But in July, the Bank of Japan (BOJ) began raising rates, strengthening the USD/JPY to ~140 (+14%), causing US markets to fall as the JPY/USD Carry Trade unwound and money left US markets. Consequently, US markets fell only to be saved in October by AI exuberance.

 

This year, the USD JPY has gained ~10% (USD/JPY is trading at 141.50), while US risk assets have declined. Is the unwinding of the JPY/USD Carry Trade and the sale of treasuries held by foreign banks creating unnatural selling pressure? We don’t know for sure, but we are watching and note that the long end of the treasury curve has been rising, which is symptomatic of foreign countries using cheaper USD to buy US reserves (treasuries). Warning: Gold will become less appealing should the USD begin to rise.
