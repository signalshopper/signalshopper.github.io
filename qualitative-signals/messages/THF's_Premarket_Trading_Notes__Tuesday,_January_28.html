It looks like a relief rally at the open after yesterday’s AI-driven tech [bubble] sell-off (S&P -1.5%, NASDAQ -3%, NVDA -17%, AVGO -17%, etc.). This morning, Chipmakers AVGO, MU, and nuclear power providers CEG and VST are higher. Offsetting the relief rally is the rise in treasury yields and the USD.

 

More EV pain for investors: Renewable energy headwinds persist after President Trump’s ‘Unleashing American Energy’ executive order called for a 90-day freeze and review of the $8 billion Energy Dept’s Office of ‘Clean Energy Demonstrations,’ of which only a fraction of the bipartisan Infrastructure/Climate Change Bill was claimed, leaving the bulk of funding vulnerable. Despite securing a $6.6B loan last week, RIVN ($12.85) is down 8.3% on the news. Buyers who bought the RIVN IPO in Nov. 2021 are down 86.7%. Other tradable US-based EV companies expected to be under pressure include TSLA, GM, F, and LCID. Note: This morning, GM reported better-than-expected results and bullish 2025 guidance.

 

Valuation: Citi strategists note how the US equity market is “getting frothier,” with the current bubble growing more comparable to past bubbles (note the green line). They aren’t ready to call the top, however. “It is always difficult to know the signal of the absolute peak in real-time.” (Citigroup)

 

 

Stay nimble: Earnings, Consumer Confidence, Wednesday’s FOMC rate decision, and Friday’s PCE numbers can be market-moving events.
