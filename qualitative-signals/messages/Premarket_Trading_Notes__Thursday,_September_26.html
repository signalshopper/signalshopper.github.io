Why is the 10-Year Treasury rising if rates are coming down? After two years of inversion – when the shorter-dated 2-Year yield rises above the longer-dated 10-Year yield -  the curve has once again steepened. This steepening is occurring despite the Federal Reserve’s recent 50 basis point rate cut and expectations for another 50 -100 bps reduction by year-end. Don’t be fooled. A rapid steepening of the yield curve after an inversion has historically signaled the start of a recession. That makes Friday’s release of the Personal Consumption Expenditures Index (PCE), the Federal Reserve’s favored measure of inflation, a data point worth watching.   

 

Expectations for Friday’s 8:30 ET PCE Index release is +0.1% headline, +0.2% core rate (excludes food and energy). If the core rate prints below 0.2%, expectations for a 50 bps rate cut will rise, and markets will respond accordingly.
