The markets look undecided at the open and ahead of tomorrow’s unemployment and wage reports.

 

As expected, the European Central Bank cut its deposit rate by 25 basis points (bps) to 3.75%, citing easing underlying inflation price pressures on the one hand (recession) and “strong domestic price pressures” due to wage growth on the other hand (stagflation?). ECB President Christine Lagarde will have some explaining to do at her 8:45 ET press conference.

 

The weekly unemployment numbers rose to a four-week high of 229K (a four-week high), but the “surprise” is attributed to the end of the school year and concurrent summertime layoffs. Treasury yields are higher on the news.
