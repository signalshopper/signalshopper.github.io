Are we there yet? Yesterday, stock futures continued to decline as growing recession concerns fueled a sell-off to start the week. The Dow dropped 2.08%, the S&P fell 2.7%, and the NASDAQ plunged 4%, with all three major indices hitting fresh multi-month lows. The S&P 500 closed at 5,614.56, down 8.6% from its record close of 6,144.15, set on Feb 19, and the tech-heavy NASDAQ declined to 13.4% from its record finish of 20,173.89, set on Dec 16.

 

Recession Concerns: Yesterday’s sell-off began after President Trump [implied] he was not an economist and declined to rule out a recession during this economic “period of transition.” What concerns us is lofty stock valuations and falling bond yields. But there is hope. Concerning valuation, we note that first-quarter earnings have been pretty good overall. So, a correction might not be a bad thing. Declining treasury yields are a little tricky.

 

We discussed this before, but an inverted yield curve (when the 10YR yield falls below the 3M yield) often foreshadows a recession (two consecutive quarters of declining GDP) in the next 12-18 months, and the Atlanta Fed’s GDP Now estimate, suggests -2.8% GDP in Q1. However, recall that Treasury yields were inverted before the money-shoveling Inflation Reduction Act, and instead of a declining stock market and GDP, markets rallied to new highs. So, if the new Administration can put money back in people’s pockets, we should be okay. If it can’t, we may be headed for a bear market. (More details to follow.)
