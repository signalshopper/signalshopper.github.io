Premarket Futures

 

The markets look to open higher after Fed Reserve Governor Chris Waller said this morning he supports more interest rate cuts this year, and he doesn’t believe tariffs will have a “significant or persistent effect on inflation.” We also note the ADP employment numbers were reported weaker than expected, perversely throwing a lifeline to rate-cut bulls. Europe is trading lower today, and we believe the US will follow their lead.

 

Yesterday’s selloff hurt. Strong growth in economic activity, plentiful jobs (8.1M openings, up from 7.8M in Dec.), and rising prices (ISM Services Purchasing Manager’s subindex rose to 54.1 versus 53.0 expected) are a recipe for potential inflation and less need for action by the Fed in the foreseeable future. The fallout? Rate cut expectations declined to one or none from three to four, 2YR Treasuries rose to 4.3% (Remember: Short-term rates reflect Fed policy), and the 10YR rose to 4.7% (the highest since Nov 2023). When treasury yields rise, risk assets usually decline.

 

Valuation: On Monday, Fed Reserve Governor Lisa Cook, one of the most dovish FOMC members,  nailed it when she said, “Valuations are elevated in a number of asset classes, including equity and corporate debt markets, where estimated risk premia are near the bottom of their historical distributions, suggesting that markets may be priced to perfection and, therefore, susceptible to large declines, which could result from bad economic news or a change in investor sentiment.”

 

Yesterday morning, Goldman Sachs said, “The S&P 500, relative to its book value and its sales, is two standard deviations above the average of the last 10 years,” and not to be outdone, Economist Robert Shiller reported his “S&P 500 cyclically adjusted price-to-equity ratio is around 37, which is near the highest level since the dot-com bubble burst.”
