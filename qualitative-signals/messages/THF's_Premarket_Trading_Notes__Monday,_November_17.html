Last Friday, after Thursday evening’s digestion of Federal Reserve Chairman Powell’s comments, i.e., rates may not come down as quickly as previously expected, the markets fell for a fourth straight day. Powell’s comments were of the usual “data-dependent” quality, but that didn’t stop institutional and retail sellers from trying to lock in this year’s profits.

 

This week, in addition to banter from six Federal Reserve speakers, consumer data points Walmart (WMT) and Lowes (LOW) report earnings on Tuesday, followed by Nvidia (NVDA) and Target (TGT) on Wednesday. Note: We don’t have the bandwidth to report on actual earnings results (see Large-Cap News flow below), but we don’t mind sharing the read-through, e.g., NVDA’s results will move AI chip stocks Taiwan Semi (TSM) and Broadcom (AVGO).  Absent a government proclamation, this week’s economic hand-wringing will begin Thursday with weekly Jobless Claims and conclude Friday with the PMI numbers and final Consumer Sentiment.

 

Valuation: According to RBC Capital Markets, “the pullback in the S&P 500 may have already started. The recent surge in 10YR Treasury yields and inflation and deficit concerns have spooked investors. While US equities can usually weather increases in 10YR yields of 250 basis points or less, last fall’s move to nearly 5% (in line with peaks seen right before the Global Financial Crisis) was enough to trigger a meaningful pullback in US equities as investor concerns are fueled by tempered expectations for Federal Reserve rate cuts and a strengthening USD, which could lead to negative earnings estimate revisions in several sectors. A 10% decline would take the S&P 500 to ~5,400 (-8%), which aligns with RBC’s trailing price-to-earnings year-end target.”

 

Not to be undone, Evercore is forecasting a decline in the S&P 500 of 5-9%, and Goldman Sachs notes that global equities have risen 40% since October 2023, leaving little room for valuation expansion.
