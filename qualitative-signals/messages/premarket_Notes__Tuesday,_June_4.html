Premarket Futures:  DOW -0.26%  S&P -0.30%  NASDAQ -0.20%  BTC -1.0%  Gold -0.62%  Crude Oil -1.78%   

 

Yesterday, stocks traded lower after the ISM Manufacturing PMI pointed to a larger-than-expected contraction in manufacturing. While weaker data increases the expectation for interest rate cuts, it also indicates that the economy is deteriorating. Banks, industrials, and other sectors that depend on economic growth led stock prices lower (a stagflation warning?). 

 

Today, the markets look to open lower on economic concerns and ahead of the 10:00 ET release of the JOLTs report.

 

Valuation: Today, we heed a warning from bearish JP Morgan Strategist Markeo Kolanovic, who said, “Profit margins are peaking, top-line growth is weakening, net interest expense is set to move back up, valuations are rich, and sentiment/positioning are near highs.” Kolanovic’s comments contrast with most strategists who expect margin expansion over the next 12 months.
