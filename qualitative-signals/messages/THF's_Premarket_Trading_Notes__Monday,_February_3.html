Markets

 

Trade Wars: The market looks oversold after the Trump Administration announced it would begin collecting new 25% tariffs on goods imported from recalcitrant Canada and Mexico starting Tuesday, along with an additional 10% tariff on Chinese goods. We believe the retaliatory bluster driven by these countries will wear off quickly when objective observers consider the following: Canadian exports to the US are 25.2% of Canada’s GDP (+75% of total exports), Mexican exports to the US are 30% of Mexico’s GDP (80% of total exports). Exports from the US into these countries are less than 6% and are offset mainly by US foreign aid. We will discuss China later.

 

This week is heavy with potentially market-moving economic news, including eight Federal Reserve speakers, production data points for January, and Friday’s significant January Jobs numbers that will test the Fed’s data-dependent/wait-and-see approach to rates.

 

Winning: The Trump Administration has placed top officials at the US Agency for International Development (USAID) and the Treasury on leave after they refused to turn over “classified material” (money trails) to Elon Musk’s government inspection teams. Apparently, with little effort, DODGE learned that both institutions look more like money laundering operations than legitimate aid operations. We anxiously await the outcome.
