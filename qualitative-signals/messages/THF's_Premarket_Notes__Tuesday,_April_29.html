Today, Investors will focus on upcoming quarterly results from Amazon, Apple, Meta, and Microsoft and their expected “tariff-dependent” guidance. Tariff Dependent: Companies will set expectations low enough to comfortably beat them over the next three quarters, e.g., GM -2% beat expectations this morning but squawked about tariff uncertainty ( ditto for UPS). As a reminder, ~180 companies report earnings this week. At 10:00 ET, we will look at April’s potential market-moving Consumer Confidence number.

 

Renewable Energy Folly: Large swaths of Spain, Portugal, and, to a lesser degree, France plunged into darkness yesterday, just six days after announcing 100% renewable energy capacity. https://www.zerohedge.com/geopolitical/spain-hit-massive-really-massive-power-blackout

 

Crypto Adoption: Mastercard is expanding support for stablecoin payments (stablecoins are cryptocurrencies pegged to stable assets like the USD). Mastercard’s stablecoin initiative spans wallet enablement, card issuance, merchant settlement, and on-chain remittances. The payments giant is working with crypto exchange OKX to roll out the “OKX Card,” which links crypto trading and Web3 activities with everyday spending. (coindesk.com)
