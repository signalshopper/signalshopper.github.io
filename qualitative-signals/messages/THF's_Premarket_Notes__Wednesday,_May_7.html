Much Ado About Nothing (Shakespeare): Stocks traded higher yesterday after "investors" parsed Richmond Fed President Tom Barkin's comments when he said, "I am optimistic that today's restrictive level of rates can take the edge off demand in order to bring inflation back to our target." But, "if the economy slows more significantly, the Federal Reserve has enough firepower to support it" (i.e., cut rates).

 

Beware of the Yield Inversion: A 2YR/10YR yield curve inversion signals an upcoming recession. Short-term bonds typically yield more than longer maturities because investors expect interest rates to remain high in the short term as the Fed battles inflation. However, capitalism works best when there is a positive return for taking more risk with lending and investments further out on the yield curve. An inverted yield curve is bad for economic activity and financial markets because higher short-term yields raise borrowing costs on consumer and commercial loans, while lower yields for long-term lending discourages risk-taking.
