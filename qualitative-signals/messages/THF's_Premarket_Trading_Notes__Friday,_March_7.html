We expect the market to trade sideways today after the unemployment numbers were reported mostly in line and until Fed Chairman Powell speaks at 2:30 ET.  

 

The Ides of March came a week early this year. Yesterday, the NASDAQ lost 483.48 points (2.6%), falling to 18,069.26, a pullback of 10.4% from its record close of 20,173.89 on December 16th. A correction is a 10% pullback from a recent high and is sometimes considered healthy. However, a correction turns into a bear market with a pullback of 20% from a recent high. We believe the market was due for a correction last October, but the AI craze drove valuations to unstable levels.   

 

Stocks fell on fears rising global trade tensions (tariffs) could spark a major economic slowdown or a recession. We remain unconvinced and possibly wrong, as previous tariff banter protected the world’s largest economy, stimulated domestic production (employment), and generated revenue for the Federal government. The strategy went sideways when President Roosevelt implemented the income tax. “And now you know the rest of the story.” (Paul Harvey) Where do we go from here? Like any overpaid Wall Street economist, we’ll tell you tomorrow.  

 

Yesterday, Atlanta Fed President Bostic noted that tariffs will likely result in higher prices for American households but also said it remains uncertain whether this will lead to increased inflation. The offset is that deregulation and energy production, are fueling business optimism. Stay tuned; the next Fed meeting is on March 18-19.
