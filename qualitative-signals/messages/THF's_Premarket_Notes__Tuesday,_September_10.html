Are we headed for a global recession? China, the world’s second-largest economy, is in trouble. Since the beginning of China’s real estate collapse about two years ago, Chinese citizens have been hoarding cash. When a country’s citizens stop or reduce spending, producers make fewer goods and employ fewer people, causing paychecks to decline and people to spend even less. The outcome is pernicious deflation. One need only look to Japan for an example of a decades-long deflationary doom loop. So we ask, can a global recession be far behind if China has an economic flu? We are watching.

The Fibonacci retracement levels are 23.60%, 38.20%, 50%, and 61.80% of the retracement of the measured move. These levels act as support on the way down and resistance on the way up. Currently, the price of Bitcoin is oscillating between resistance at 23.60% ($60 K) and 38.20% ($51.5 K). BTC traders may want to sell into rallies until BTC begins to break the Fibonacci retracement levels. Note BTC’s descending channel. (Source: Investopedia)
