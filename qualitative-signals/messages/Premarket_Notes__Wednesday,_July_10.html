Powell Redux today? Yesterday, Powell stuck to the party line, i.e., inflation is still not low enough for the Federal Reserve to begin its cutting cycle. However, the central bank does consider overtightening risks, mainly because the labor market is not overheated. Then he said while the Fed doesn't like to "take things off the table, it doesn't seem likely that the next policy move would be a rate increase."

 

Will we encounter the same obfuscation later this morning when Powell speaks to the House? If so, traders may interpret Powell's reticence as a signal that Thursday's CPI and Friday's PPI may print hotter than expected tomorrow.

 

Recession Watch: Based on recent economic indicators, the Atlanta Fed's GDPNow gauge of growth in the second quarter slipped to a 1.5% annual rate from earlier projections closer to 3%.
