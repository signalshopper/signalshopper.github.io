Markets look to open higher as bad news is good news for rate bulls. The Chicago Fed National Activity Index fell to -0.34 in July 2024 from a revised -0.09 in June, compared with market expectations of +0.03. Unemployment claims rose by 4 K from the previous week to 232 K, slightly ahead of market expectations of 230,000 (a three-week high), and yesterday, the jobs numbers were revised by 818 K. What could go wrong?

 

Yesterday, markets rose after the FOMC minutes indicated the Federal Reserve is more inclined to lower the Fed Funds rate at the September meeting (especially after we learned 818 K previously reported jobs were fictitious). Is this another recession signal? About 358 K of these fictitious jobs were in the higher paying business services, 76 K in financial activities, and 68 K in technology. According to layoffs.fyi, there were more than 165 K layoffs in tech in 2022, over 260 K in 2023, and 130 K in 2024. The data is in, and the economy appears to be slowing.
