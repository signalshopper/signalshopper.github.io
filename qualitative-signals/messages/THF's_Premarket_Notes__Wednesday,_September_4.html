A Recession Signal? Yesterday, we learned that the manufacturing sector is continuing to shrink. The ISM Manufacturing Index for August was 47.2% versus 47.9% expected and below the 50% needed for expansion. The new orders index fell 2.8% to 44.6%, the lowest level in more than two years, and the production barometer declined 1.1% to 44.8%, the lowest level since May 2020. Economists believe the road to prosperity is lower rates and a conclusive general election, i.e., clear economic policy.   

 

Has a Rate Cut Already Occurred? Investors will look closely at Friday’s August jobs report, which should help determine how much the Fed will reduce the Fed Funds rate, currently 5.25%-%.5% at the September FOMC meeting. Here’s the rub. The 10-YR treasury yield is down 44 bps since the end of July, implying that a rate cut for borrowing purposes has already occurred. We note: Wall Street expects the US to add 160K jobs in August vs. July’s lower-than-expected 114K rise.
