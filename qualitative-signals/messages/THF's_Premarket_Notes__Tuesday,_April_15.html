  • * “Treasuries are showing signs of losing their traditional safe-haven status amid growing unease over the direction of US economic policy.” THF analysis: Yada, yada, yada.

Currencies: DXY 99.741 (+0.108)

  • * The USD strengthened after the Administration announced its decision to exempt certain technology products from reciprocal tariffs and suggested a potential pause in the proposed 25% auto import levies.

 

Precious Metal Futures:  Gold 3,326 (+9.94)  Silver 32.29 (+0.11)  Copper 4.564 (-0.061)

  • * Gold rose as uncertainty over President Trump’s tariff plans continued to drive safe-haven demand. Also supporting the precious metal, Federal Reserve Governor Christopher Waller said on Monday that “interest rates may need to be cut if Trump’s large-sized tariffs remain in place.” Waller also said tariffs are “transitory.”

 

Energy Futures:  WTI 60.98 (-0.54)  Brent 64.33 (-0.55)  Nat Gas 3.241 (-0.084)  Gasoline RBOB 2.0189 (-0.004)

  • * Supply/Demand: OPEC+ cut its demand growth forecasts for 2025 (1.3M bpd from 1.45M) and 2026 (1.28M bpd from 1.43M), citing slower first-quarter trends and US trade tariffs. We also note that nuclear talks between the US and Iran raise the prospect of increased oil exports from Iran.
