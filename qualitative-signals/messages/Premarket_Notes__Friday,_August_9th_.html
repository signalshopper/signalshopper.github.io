Markets

 

Keeping up with the market’s multiple, unsettled personalities is difficult. Yesterday, a better-than-expected jobless claims report reduced fears of a recession and stoked enthusiasm for risk assets. While bets that the Federal Reserve will deliver multiple rate cuts this year remain intact, markets remain divided on how aggressively the central bank will lower borrowing costs. Wherefore art thou, Goldilocks?

 

Today, the markets look muted-to-down at the open as a lack of catalysts after yesterday’s strong performance leaves traders without a roadmap. We note that some profit-taking of the high-flying tech stocks seems to be pressuring the S&P 500. Like an anxious dog chasing a meatless bone, Asia and the EU are following yesterday’s markets in the US.
