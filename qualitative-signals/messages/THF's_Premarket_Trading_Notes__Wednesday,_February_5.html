Markets look to open lower, led by weak earnings reports and a stronger-than-expected ADP private jobs report. 

 

Stay tuned: The Fed has a dual mandate of maintaining a strong economy and labor market without increasing inflation. The Fed’s preferred measure of inflation is the 12-month change in the personal consumption expenditures (PCE) price index. The PCE peaked at 7.2% in June 2022 and fell to 2.6% in December last year. Core PCE inflation (excluding food and energy) peaked at 5.6% in 2022 and declined to 2.8% in December last year. If the goal is to bring inflation down to 2.0% and stimulate the economy (jobs) with lower rates, then the Administration will have to find a way to reign in inflationary spending or reduce costs. Stay tuned: On Friday, we will learn the US Employment rate for January. A higher-than-expected number will dash hopes for forthcoming rate cuts.
