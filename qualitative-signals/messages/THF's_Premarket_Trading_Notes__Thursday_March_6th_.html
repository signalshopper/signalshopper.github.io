Houston, We Have A Problem: The recession jury is out, but “the GDP Now model estimate for real GDP growth (seasonally adjusted annual rate) in the first quarter of 2025 is -2.8%, down from -1.5% on Feb 28. After Monday morning’s releases from the US Census Bureau and the Institute for Supply Management, the nowcast of 1Q real personal consumption expenditures growth and real private fixed investment growth fell from 1.3% and 3.5% to 0.0%% and 0.1%.”

 

Will Growth Continue To Decline? “US. financial conditions have been weakening all year. Weakness caused a 10% S&P 500 correction in 2023 and a nearly 20% decline in the Magnificent Seven stocks last summer. When consumers are cautious, they spend less on discretionary goods than everyday staples like toothpaste. This reaction is reflected in the relative price performance of these stock groups. (https://www.atlantafed.org/cqer/research/gdpnow)

 

 

On A Positive Note: “No one has abused their balance sheets in this cycle to the point where they are vulnerable.” The USD has rolled over, and bond yields are coming down. Both trends help spur growth. The Fed will go into easing mode later this year because of the economic growth slowdown, which will take inflation down to 2% and give the Fed cover to ease rates.”.  (Jim Paulsen of Paulsen Perspectives )
