The markets look to open lower with limited economic news, a slew of quarterly earnings reports, and tomorrow’s weekly jobless claims, flash services, and manufacturing PMIs. As we continue to note, risk appetite is declining ahead of the election, and this negativity should continue to put pressure on stocks, especially the high-beta tech and small-caps, as Treasury yields continue to rise.   

 

Treasuries Partie Deux: Yesterday, we discussed rates and why they may not trend lower near term. Today, we wanted to dig deeper and add the compensation investors require for taking duration risk. If fixed-income investors are, one, unsure of the election outcome and the higher deficits (inflation) either candidate may bring, and two, they also believe the economy is stronger than expected (lowering expectations for a 50 bps rate reduction), they will require higher yields for holding longer-dated debt (10YR, 30YR treasuries). We have seen this play out over the last 1-2 weeks. Today, the 10YR is trading at its highest level in three months.
