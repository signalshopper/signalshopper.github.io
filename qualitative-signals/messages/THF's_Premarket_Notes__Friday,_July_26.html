Markets

 

Premarket Futures:  DOW +0.60%  S&P +0.69%  NASDAQ +0.93%  10YR -2bps  Gold +0.85%  BTC +4.80%  Oil -0.79%

 

Despite core PCE prices rising faster than expected (2.0% versus 1.0% expected month/month), markets look to open higher (relief rally) after June's PCE year/year was a reported 2.6% (unchanged) but higher than the 2.50% expectation.    

 

Valuation: SPX (5428) bounced off support at 5,500 on July 19. on July 24, SPX broke down through the entire support area at 5,440-5,490 and thus established a lower low by doing so. We now have a pattern of lower highs and lower lows, the hallmark of a bearish market. A move back above 5,585 would be bullish, but the new trend is bearish, at least for the short term. (McMillan Analysis)  This week, the S&P is -1.9%, the NASDAQ -3.1%, and the DOW -0.0%.

 

Global Recession: We recently discussed the slowdown in Asia (China, India, etc.). Yesterday, the Eurozone composite PMI (manufacturing and services sector) declined to 50.1 in July from 50.9 in June and was lower than expectations of 51.1, the worst since February. Germany's manufacturing sector, the economic manufacturing engine for the EU, contributed the most to the slowdown, where the PMI of 42.6 was lower than the previous 43.5 and forecasts of 44.1. With Germany's service sector slowing from a PMI reading of 53.1 to 52, the overall private sector German economy was in contraction with a reading of 48.7. (Is the EU in recession? Are rate cuts to follow?).
