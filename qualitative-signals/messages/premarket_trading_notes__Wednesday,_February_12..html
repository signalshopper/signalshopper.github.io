Stubborn inflation (see economic reports below) is pressuring markets this morning, which helps explain yesterday’s cautious remarks from Fed Chairman Powell. All measures came in above forecasts, with headline inflation rising to 3% and core increasing to 3.3%. 

 

Interestingly, Chairman Powell emphasized patience yesterday after the Fed cut rates at its final three meetings of 2024, lowering borrowing costs by a percentage point ahead of the Presidential election. Those cuts occurred in the lead-up to the presidential election, when economic concerns were front and center for voters. Did the Fed juice the economy to help Democrat incumbents? Now, with Trump in the White House and the economy performing better than expected, the Fed decided to pause its rate-cutting cycle. A straw man might wonder if politics played a role in the Fed’s decision.
