Recession Watch: "McDonald's, Coke, Nestle, Starbucks, and Pepsi have all now flagged that low-income consumers are no longer able to absorb inflation. McDonald's reported same-store sales are down 3%, and transactions dropped 7%. Pepsi reported a 5% drop in sales. Nestle had an 8% drop for Hot Pockets, frozen pizzas and Stouffer frozen dinners. Starbucks is losing the low end altogether, with its stock plunging 14% after turning in lower revenue, transactions, and ticket size, while losing 1.5 million loyalty-reward users. Nestle's CFO estimates the loss in discretionary purchasing power among low-income since Biden took office has come to roughly half. (Economist, Peter St Onge)
