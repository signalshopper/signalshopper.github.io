Is the Federal Reserve losing its mojo? Last week, despite the Hawkish tone set by the Fed, treasury rates declined (-21 bps) after a softer-than-expected CPI, a decline in PPI, and a rise in Jobless claims revived hope for more than one rate cut in the Fed Funds rate (currently 5.25% - 5.50%). We are watching the tug-of-war between above-target inflation and the needs of the Administration ahead of the November election. Note: Rate cut hopeism favors small-cap companies (lower borrowing rates), hence the uptick in the Russell 2000 (ETF: IWM is +1% YTD, +23% since last Nov)

 

Bitcoin Price Discovery: A cash-and-carry trade, also known as a basis trade, is a market-neutral strategy that exploits the price discrepancy between an asset and its corresponding derivatives. Traders buy BTC or BTC ETFs and short bitcoin futures (available since Dec 2017) while they wait for the cash and futures prices to converge. Traders will hold BTC/BTC ETFs through the futures delivery date and use them to cover their short trades. It's believed that a portion of the BTC ETF demand, which helped drive BTC prices to $73,079 in March, was driven by traders putting on these basis/carry trades.

 

When BTC ETFs were approved, the spread between BTC's cash price and BTC futures was ~28 basis points (~28%). Today, the spread is ~7%. A riskless 8-week treasury bill returns ~5.37%, leaving a profit margin of less than 2%. Therefore, we can conclude that BTC's run-up to $73K was partly due to the scalping of the cash/futures price mismatch. If this is correct, excluding an unknown catalyst, near-term technical resistance of $70K makes sense. (Data Source: Distributed Ledger)
