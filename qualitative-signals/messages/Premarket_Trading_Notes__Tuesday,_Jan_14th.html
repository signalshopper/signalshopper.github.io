Random Thoughts

 

Large-cap bank earnings begin this week with JPM, GS, WFC, and C reporting Wednesday and BAC and MS reporting Thursday.

Expectations are for good fourth-quarter results followed by weaker-than-expected guidance as banks dependent upon M&A activity will be impaired by the Fed’s reluctance to continue reducing rates.

 

Is it a coincidence that the S&P 500’s peak on December 6 coincided with the trough in 10YR Treasury yields?

 

At 2:00 ET today, the important but often overlooked Federal Reserve’s Beige Book will be released. “The Beige Book describes current economic conditions and prospects based on information gathered from the 12 Fed districts, giving economists and policymakers insight about local and regional inflation, hiring, employment, and manufacturing conditions.”

 

Bond Vigilantes (Part 3): The rest of the world is catching on to our thesis, i.e., bond buyers are mad as hell and not going to take it anymore. “The risk-off tone is that central banks are losing control of the bond market. Yields on the long end of the yield curve have been rising despite the general tone of monetary easing by central banks worldwide. It isn’t only US Treasury yields. Yields in Europe have also risen, and the carnage in UK gilts has been particularly acute.” (Barrons)
