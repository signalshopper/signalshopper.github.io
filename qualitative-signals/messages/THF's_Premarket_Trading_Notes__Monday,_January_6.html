Premarket Futures:  DOW +0.80%  SPX +1.26%  NASDAQ +1.77%  10YR +1 bp  Gold -7.41  Oil +0.61%  BTC $99,227

 

Markets look to open higher after it was reported Trump’s Administration is expected to be more targeted/less inflationary in its approach to tariffs. Targets identified include the defense industrial chain, critical medical supplies, and energy production. Stocks in Europe are higher on the news. In the US, large-cap technology stocks, followed by the market cap weighted (large-cap tech), SPY are moving the most.  

 

This week, markets expect the FOMC to announce a Fed Funds rate reduction of 25bps to 4.25%-4.5% at 2:00 ET, Wednesday. The debate is whether or not the Fed will stick with four 25 bps cuts this year or, as many suspect, reduce that number to two rate cuts. Powell has said the Fed will not bake in Trump’s tariff proposals (deemed inflationary), but he has been fickle lately. Market-moving economic reports include Wednesday’s ADP Employment Report and Friday’s December Employment reports.
