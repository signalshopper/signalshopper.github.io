Markets look to open lower as traders speculate on the President’s tariff announcement after the close. This bearishness continues despite this morning’s bullish ADP data that showed private employers added 155,000 jobs in March (versus expectations of 105,000), with both the service and goods-producing sectors posting gains and an upwardly revised February result.

 

Are we headed for a recession? Widening credit spreads - the premium bond investors’ demand to hold on to riskier corporate debt/high-yield bonds over treasuries – is a recession signal. As of last week, the credit spread hit its highest level since August (+3.4%), but it remains extremely tight, implying corporates are still a safe bet and stocks should remain attractive (relative to treasuries).
