Treasuries: Declining yields are “usually” positive for risk assets, but the President’s stated goal to balance the budget during his term, despite current deficits of ~7% of GDP, is causing expectations of steep cuts to public expenditure to increase, pushing treasury yields lower.

 

Bubbles: Perennial but successful stock market bear Jeremy Grantham, co-founder of GMO, notes that “every measure of traditional value for stocks, including the cyclically adjusted price-to-earnings ratio,” is at record levels and that includes AI stocks, the market’s main driver since last October. Great minds think alike, but where our view diverges from Grantham is his belief that the Chinese and European markets will outperform the US for the next decade. One, we believe the global economy depends on the world’s largest consumer (recall the US bailed out foreign banks during the ’08 global mortgage meltdown), and two, the structural problems in these foreign markets, both political and economic, are unlikely to be resolved anytime soon.    

 

Slowing Economy: “Personal consumption expenditures (PCE) in the United States fell by 0.2% month-on-month in January 2025, following an upwardly revised 0.8% increase in December and missing market expectations of a 0.1% gain. This marked the first decline in consumer spending since March 2023, primarily driven by a drop in spending on goods (-1.2% vs. 1.2% in December). Spending on durable goods plummeted by 3.0% (vs. 1.3% in December) while spending on nondurable goods edged down by 0.2% (vs. 1.2% in December). Additionally, spending on services increased at a slower pace of 0.3%, compared to 0.7% the previous month.”
