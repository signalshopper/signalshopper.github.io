It’s another risk-off day as “investors” continue to react to the rise in Treasury yields and the USD.

 

Last week was not a good week for the indexes, with the Dow falling 1.9%, the S&P 500 1.9%, the NASDAQ 2.3%, and the Russell 2000 3.5%. Compounding the problem is the relentless increase of 10 and 30 YR Treasury yields, despite a 100 bps decline in the Fed Funds rate since September (4.25% - 4.50% from a 16-year high of 5.25% - 5.50%). As discussed last Friday, we believe the Bond Vigilantes/rational investors/sovereign nations are sensing higher US inflation ahead and remain reluctant to take on risk at the longer end of the curve (10 and 30YR Treasuries). As a result, the equity markets look like they will continue to correct to more reasonable valuations.

 

This week, we will receive inflation confirmation one way or the other. The December Producer Price Index will be released on Tuesday, the Consumer Price Index on Wednesday, and Retail Sales and Jobless Claims on Thursday. (see Economic Reports below) Any upside surprise could close the door on further rate cuts.
