Yesterday, the Bulls took a breather until early afternoon. There was no news, just consolidation and profit-taking ahead of today’s CPI. Today, stocks look to open higher after the October inflation reports were, once again, “exactly” in line with expectations. The news leaves room for the Federal Reserve to continue cutting rates in December.

 

A Shout-Out to President-Elect DJ Trump as Economic Optimism Hits a 39-month High

Yesterday, the  RealClearMarkets/TIPP Economic Optimism Index, a key measure of US consumer confidence, jumped 13.4% in Nov to 53.2, the highest in over three years. This marks the first time the index has been positive since Sept 2021, ending a 38-month negative streak, spurred by President Donald Trump’s return to office. US consumer inflation expectations for the year ahead edged down to 2.9% in Oct 2024, the lowest since Oct 2020, from 3% in each of the previous four months.

 

Valuation: According to Birinyi Associates data cited by the Wall Street Journal, “the S&P 500’s price-to-earnings ratio -- a gauge of earnings estimates for the next 12 months -- stood at 25.37 last Friday, up from 19.69 a year ago.”
