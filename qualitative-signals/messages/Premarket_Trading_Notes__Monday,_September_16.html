This roller coaster of a market may come to rest Wednesday afternoon at 2:00 ET when the FOMC releases its Fed Funds rate decision and Fed Chairman Powell, in his obfuscating manner, explains how the Fed arrived at its decision. Expectations are for a 25 bps rate reduction. Fans of a larger rate reduction need to beware. A 50 bps reduction would signal fears of persistent inflation and despair. Will the market “sell on the news” after last week’s massive rally (NASDAQ +6%, S&P 500 +4%, DOW +3%)?

 

A US Debt Bomb: “Interest payments on the national debt are now larger than the costs of Medicare and the Defense budget combined. Therefore, the national debt may contribute to further inflation.” “This is the first time in American history that interest payments on the national debt have risen to $1.2 T. ‘So, it’s hard to look at it and say, ’Well, no, we’re out of the woods’. I don’t think so.” (JPM CEO, Jamie Dimon)

 

If we do the calculus, i.e., slowing employment and manufacturing plus the inflationary pressures of higher deficits and infrastructure spending. We can conclude that a recession may have already arrived, and stagflation, the worst possible outcome, is not off the table.

 

Pernicious Deflation: China’s recent economic data shows that the country’s supportive measures failed to spur a meaningful recovery in the property sector as new home prices fell 5.30% Y/Y, its fastest pace since May 2015, after falling 4.90% in July. Prices fell for the 14th month as the property market deals with debt-ridden developers, incomplete constructions, and declining buyer confidence. (Reuters) Data released over the weekend showed China’s industrial output and consumption (deflation) continue to decline (a negative demand signal for global energy prices).
