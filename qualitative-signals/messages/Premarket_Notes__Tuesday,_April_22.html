Premarket Futures:  DOW +1.075  SPX +1.05%  NASDAQ +1.17%  10YR -3 bps  Gold +0.77%  Oil +1.0%  BTC +2.0%

 

Yesterday, speculation from the White House about potentially removing Powell further unsettled markets, raising alarms over the Fed’s independence and eroding confidence in US assets. Today, the indexes look to open higher on what appears to be an oversold market, a signal confirmed by declining Treasury yields and a lower USD.

 

Notable: National Economic Council Director Kevin Hassett said Friday that the Fed under Powell, who Trump appointed during his first term, had acted politically to benefit Democrats. 

 

“The policy of this Federal Reserve was to raise rates the minute President Trump was elected last time, to say that the supply-side tax cuts were going to be inflationary,” Hassett said, adding that Fed officials opted not to go “on TV and at IMF meetings and warn about the terrible inflation from the obvious runaway spending from Joe Biden, and the obvious runaway spending from Joe Biden was textbook inflationary,” “And then they cut rates right ahead of the election.”

 

Recession Betting Odds: The betting market, Polymarket, indicates a 37% chance of a US recession this year. There is also speculation that the Administration is engineering a recession to weaken the dollar and force the Fed to lower rates. We doubt the Administration is that nefarious.
