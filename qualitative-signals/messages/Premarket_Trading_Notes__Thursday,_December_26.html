Premarket Futures: DOW -0.35%  S&P -0.28%  NASDAQ -0.23%  Gold +0.25%  Oil +0.81%

 

The exchanges are open today and tomorrow, but participation is historically lower than average this time of the year. Markets look to open lower after Tuesday’s pre-Christmas rally amid concerns heading into the New Year, including the ticking debt bomb, inflation worries driven by talk of tariffs and tax cuts, and a less generous Federal Reserve as the economy continues to chug along. Treasuries are trending higher (usually negative for risk assets), and the mega-caps, which rallied Tuesday, look weaker at the open (AAPL, TSLA, AMZN, MSFT, and META). Note: The S&P has risen 28.4% this year, but without the Magnificent-7 stocks, the S&P would only have risen 13.2%.
