Yesterday, markets rallied at the open, believing that US-China negotiations were progressing. Then, a few hours after the market opened, Treasury Secretary Bessent said that talks with China had not started. This morning, markets are muted and, we believe, are awaiting earnings releases.

 

A BTC Bull Run? It’s not surprising that BTC’s price tends to rise when the global money supply (global M2) increases – subject to variables such as ETF inflows, halving, macro policy surprises, etc. What’s interesting is the 90-day lag between M2 and BTC‘s price.

 

According to several crypto analysts, BTC looks poised for a 3-4 month run (from April 7 to May/July) if the correlation holds. Price targets range from ~$133k to $140k. (Sources: CryptoSlate, Bitcoinist, TradingView, et al.)
