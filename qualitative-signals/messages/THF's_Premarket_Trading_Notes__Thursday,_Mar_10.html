Despite softer-than-expected inflation data, the market looks to take profits at the open after yesterday’s historic rally, and lingering concerns about the President’s 10% universal tariff continue to cast a shadow.

 

Valuation: Stock futures rose yesterday in a historic rally after President Trump announced a 90-day pause on his reciprocal tariffs for countries that have not retaliated. The DOW rose 7.87%, the SPX 9.52%, and the RUT 8.66% for their largest one-day gains in five years. The NASDAQ rose 12.16% for its best single-day since 2001. So, where do we go from here?

 

Yesterday, the SPX traded above its support level of 5,400 (currently 5,326 premarket) and, depending upon today’s and tomorrow’s economic news, looks to be working towards its first resistance level of 5,770 (+5.4%).

 

FOMC Minutes: The Fed’s policymakers expected inflation to be pushed higher this year due to the impact of elevated tariffs. However, they acknowledged considerable uncertainty around the magnitude and persistence of these effects. At the same time, most officials highlighted the possibility that inflationary pressures from various sources could prove more persistent than previously anticipated. Nearly all participants viewed inflation risks as tilted to the upside, while employment risks were skewed to the downside. The Fed kept the federal funds rate unchanged at 4.25%-4.5% during its March 2025 meeting, extending the pause in its rate-cut cycle that began in January, in line with expectations. The Fed also raised their expectations for inflation for 2025 and 2026 and downgraded their 2025 growth forecasts. The Fed maintained its expectations for a rate reduction of 50 bps this year, which contrasts with the investors who recently lowered their expectations from four rate cuts of 25 bps to three (paraphrased from the Fed’s release).
