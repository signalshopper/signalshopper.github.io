The unemployment rate in the US declined to 4.1% in December from 4.2% in November, below market expectations of 4.2%. Meanwhile, the labor force participation rate remained at 62.5%, and the employment rate rose to 60% from 59.8%. Don’t be fooled. Historically, temporary retail employment almost always rises in December and falls in January. Regardless of the facts, expectations for a rate cut just went out the window. Stocks will follow Europe lower at the open. Looking forward, if the SPY can’t hold 580, we may be looking at the start of a downtrend.    

 

Send in the Bond Vigilantes: Bond vigilantes/rational investors act to impose fiscal responsibility when government institutions won’t. These economic bond vigilantes sell significant amounts of government bonds, driving prices down and yields up, making it fiscally irresponsible for the Treasury/Administration to take on more debt. We saw the results of these actions during the Clinton and Obama administrations. In effect, the bond vigilantes do the job the [politically motivated] Federal Reserve is unwilling to do.

 

We are beginning to consider the prevalence of bond vigilantes in light of the frivolous Biden Administration and ahead of the Trump Administration’s tariffs and tax cuts as treasury yields are at one-year highs despite a 100 bps/1.0% decline in the Fed Funds rate. Rising rates raise borrowing costs, making investing in stocks less desirable, especially small-cap stocks, debt-laden countries (see Argentina, EU,  UK, etc.), and emerging markets. We can discuss the effect of rising bond yields on the USD and the negative pressure it puts on exports and foreign economies later.
