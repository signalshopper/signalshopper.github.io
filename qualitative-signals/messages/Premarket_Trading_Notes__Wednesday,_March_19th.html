Markets are showing a relief rally at the open as investors await the FOMC’s 2:00 ET rate decision and conference call, followed by Fed Chairman Powell’s 2:30 ET remarks.

 

We question if the market’s correction is due to tariff banter? Recent data points reflect a structural/economic shift in the economy away from [gov’t] stimulus-driven demand to supply-driven expansion. Consumer prices declined in February, with CPI falling to 2.8% year/year, down from 3.0% in January. Core CPI (excludes food and energy), viewed as a better measure of underlying inflation, has declined to its lowest level since April 2021. As measured by the PPI, producer prices remained flat (versus expectations for a 0.30% increase). The trend is clear: production is expanding, and inflationary pressures are easing.  

 

Consumer spending and business sentiment have shown signs of caution, but the industrial and housing sectors tell a story of expansion and renewed confidence. Industrial production surged 0.7% in February (0.2% expected). The increase pushed total industrial output to an all-time high. Manufacturing output rose 0.9%, the strongest gain in a year, with an 8.5% surge in auto production. Housing starts rose 1.2% in February to an annualized pace of 1.5 m, the highest level in two years (1.38 m expected). Single-family home construction rose 11.4% to an annualized 1.11 m—the fastest pace since early 2023. Mining output, including oil and natural gas extraction, rose 2.8%, reflecting renewed investment in US energy production, and the US is now the world’s largest net exporter of natural gas.

 

“Returning to production marks a fundamental departure from the stimulus-fueled, consumption-driven model that defined the past decade. This shift also explains the stock market’s recent volatility, as investors recalibrate their portfolios from the investment strategies built around Biden’s unsustainable deficit policies toward the Trump economy.” (paraphrased from a Breitbart Business Digest research note)
