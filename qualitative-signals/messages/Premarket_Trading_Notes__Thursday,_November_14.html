In a sign of economic weakness (hidden from the BLS’ economic reports), Advance Auto Parts (APP, -4.7%) announced it would “close 523 corporate stores, exit 204 independent locations, and shutter four distribution centers by mid-2025.” APP cited inflationary headwinds and competition from China as a primary reason for its tepid 2024 performance (stagflation signal?).  

 

Bitcoin: Are we there yet? BTC is currently in price-discovery mode. This process concludes when almost all BTC’s circulating supply finds buyers at a price (like day one of an IPO offering). “Historically, such BTC phases have lasted an average of ~22 days before a major pullback, when ~5% of the circulating supply could be pushed below the original acquisition price. The current rally has remained at a high-profit level for about 12 consecutive days. The current cost basis for new or short-term investors is ~$66,800, putting the upper and lower statistical bands (plus or minus one standard deviation) at around $94,900 and $51,600.” (Glassnode) If the Glassnode analysts are correct, we have about ten more days of trading madness before buyers and sellers determine a fair price (until the next catalyst).
