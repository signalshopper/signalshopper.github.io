Premarket Futures:  DOW  S&P  NASDAQ  10YR  Gold BTC Oil

 

My apologies. I neglected to alert everyone to my travel plans. I spent last week in Miami and watched from the sidelines as the indexes fell. That aside, readers likely recognized our bearish, recession-fearful commentary over the past few months. Overnight, Asian markets were tanking on fears that the world’s largest economy and the proverbial ‘last man standing’ might be on the brink of a recession. Are investors taking profits because of the fear of another Middle East War, the never-ending Ukraine conflict, and a global recession (see China, India, and the EU), or is this just seasonal weakness? If rate cuts are intended to get an economy out of a recession, then we are already in one, right? We see shoals ahead.      

 

Is the negative reaction to Fed rate cuts reasonable? The answer may be yes. As the chart below demonstrates, the last three recessions followed rate cuts (‘90, ‘00, and ‘08).

 

 

On the flip side, a recession happened after the last five times inflation went above 5% in 1970, 1974, 1980, 1990, and 2008.
