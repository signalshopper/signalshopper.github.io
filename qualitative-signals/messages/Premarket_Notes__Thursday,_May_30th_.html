US GDP (1.3%) expanded by less than reported in the first quarter, moving further away from expectations of higher growth, challenging the perception of a resilient economy and slightly alleviating concerns of inflationary pressures. Initial jobless claims remained above average for the year (perversely positive). The news is positive for rate doves (Treasury rates and the USD are lower). We believe the deflationary cloud will cause anxiety and caution in traders ahead of tomorrow’s PCE inflation data.

 

Note: Despite the runup in stock prices, corporate profits fell by 1.7% from the previous period to $2.754.5 T in the first quarter, from a 3.9% increase in the fourth quarter, missing forecasts for a 3.9% rise.
