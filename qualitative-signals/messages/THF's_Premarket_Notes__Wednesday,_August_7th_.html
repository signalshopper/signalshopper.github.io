Asian markets are higher after the BOJ said it wouldn't raise rates, relieving fears of the yen carry trade unwinding. (A strategy where traders are incentivized to borrow inexpensive yen and buy higher-yielding assets elsewhere).

 

Yesterday, the institutions, not retail investors, bought the dip as expectations for a recession and an emergency Fed Funds rate cut decline. That may change after weekly jobless claims are released tomorrow morning.

 

So, upward and onward we go!

 

Caution: According to the Federal Reserve Bank of New York's Quarterly Report on Household Debt and Credit, 10.93% of credit card accounts were delinquent by more than 90 days, the highest since the 1Q of 2012. Auto loan delinquencies of 90+ days hit 4.43%, the highest level since the first quarter of 2021.
