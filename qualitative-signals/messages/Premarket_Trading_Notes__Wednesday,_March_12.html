Let’s review: The January JOLTS report highlighted the beginning of a potential blue-collar boom, particularly in manufacturing, as job openings and hires rose significantly in the early weeks of the new Administration. The latest S&P Global Manufacturing Purchasing Managers’ Index (PMI) also registered its strongest growth since June 2022. So, where’s the beef? The Administration’s policies appear to be stimulating manufacturers to invest more in domestic production, strengthening America’s industrial base, and that’s a good thing, right?

  • * The annual inflation rate in the US eased to 2.8% in Feb from 3% in Jan, below forecasts of 2.9%. Energy costs declined 0.2% year-on-year, following a 1% rise in Jan which was the first increase in 6 months. Gasoline (-3.1% vs -0.2%) and fuel oil (-5.1% vs -5.3%) were lower while natural gas prices soared (6% vs 4.9%). Inflation also slowed for shelter (4.2% vs 4.4%), used cars and trucks (0.8% vs 1%), transportation (6% vs 8%) while prices continued to fall for new vehicles (-0.3% vs -0.3%). On the other hand, inflation accelerated for food (2.6% vs 2.5%). On a monthly basis, the CPI rose by 0.2%, down from January’s 0.5% increase which was the highest monthly inflation rate since August 2023, and compared to forecasts of 0.3%.
