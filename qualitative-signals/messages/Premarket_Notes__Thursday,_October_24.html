We witnessed quite a meltdown yesterday (DOW -0.96%, S&P -0.92%, NASDAQ -1.60%) after Treasury yields spiked to three-month highs and negative earnings releases outweighed positive earnings surprises. After yesterday’s close, TSLA (+14%) reported an earnings beat on lower-than-expected revenues but said the company expects deliveries to be 20-30% higher next year, and the market cheered (yikes), but still, the news is bullish for large-cap tech stocks and the NASDAQ). Today’s markets are likely to be driven by more corporate results.

 

Today, the market will bid up stocks on the better-than-expected unemployment numbers (claims declined 15k from last week), declining treasury yields, and a weaker USD.

 

BCA Research found a correlation between the Yen carry trade and the rise in tech stocks. Technology stocks fell in August when markets believed the carry trade (free money) was over, but with the USD/JPY up 9% since mid-September, the NASDAQ 100 (QQQ) rallied 12%. “As US rate cut expectations declined, US bond prices fell and, paradoxically, US  tech valuations re-inflated. The reason is that, in a mirror image to the Aug. 2 jobs report, real rates in Japan versus the US became more negative again, reigniting flows into US tech, funded by yen borrowing.” We are watching.
