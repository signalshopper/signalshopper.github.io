New tariff threats continue to fuel inflation fears after Trump’s announced plans to impose auto tariffs of around 25%, along with similar duties on semiconductors and pharmaceutical imports. We believe the market will trade in a channel until the 2:00 ET FOMC Minutes release.

 

Home Builders: Yesterday, the [sales-driven] Home-builder confidence number fell to its lowest in five months, 42 from 47. The decline is attributed to unusually high mortgage rates (6.87%) and potential tariffs on imported building materials from Canada, China, and Mexico, which could drive up prices by as much as $17,000 to $22,000. We note that the construction of new homes adds more than 2.0% to the annual GDP in the US.  Note: Toll Brothers (TOL) fell 6% after missing Q1 earnings. (See US Housing Starts below.)

 

Electric Vehicles: Another one bites the dust as Nikola (NKLA, -49%) files for Chapter 11. That leaves US EV makers TSLA ($354.11) and government-sponsored RIVN ($14.41) in the market. As an aside, HSBC is ditching its target of reaching net-zero carbon emissions across its business by 2030 by 20 years. (Reuters)
