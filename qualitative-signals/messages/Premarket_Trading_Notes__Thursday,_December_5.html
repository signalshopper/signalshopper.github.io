The index futures are mostly unchanged after yesterday’s record highs and ahead of Friday’s employment data. At 12:15 ET, slightly bearish Richmond Fed President Tom Barkin speaks. Treasury yields are higher (usually negative for risk assets).  

 

Premarket Futures:  DOW -0.09%  S&P -0.04%  NASDAQ -0.06%  10YR +4 bps  Gold -0.24  Oil +0.57%  BTC $102,900

 

Yesterday, Federal Reserve Chair Powell signaled support for a slower pace of interest-rate cuts when he said the economy was stronger than the central bank had expected in September. San Francisco Fed President Mary Daly said there was “no sense of urgency” on reducing borrowing costs further. Their comments did not affect the market’s exuberance. Our next data point will be Friday’s release of the November jobs report.  

 

Bullish Moves at the SEC: President-Elect DJ Trump made another bullish move for the $3.5T crypto market last night when he announced plans to nominate crypto bull Paul Atkins as Chairman of the SEC. The move replaces the reviled Gary Gensler, who is responsible for the agency’s regulation-by-enforcement approach to the industry and pushed BTC through its $100k resistance level. Atkins served as SEC commissioner in 2002-08 under President G. W. Bush and was critical of some reforms that emerged from the global financial crisis in 2008. Specifically, he criticized the Dodd-Frank legislation as too burdensome on the banking industry. We suspect bank chairmen and women are high-fiving this morning.
