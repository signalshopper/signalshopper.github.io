Markets

 

Premarket Futures:  DOW +0.31%  S&P +0.51%  NASDAQ +0.95%  10YR -1 bp  Gold +0.29%  Oil –0.24%  BTC $104,042

 

The NASDAQ and SPX are moving higher on AI enthusiasm (NVDA +3%, ORCL +9%, MSFT +2%, NVDA +3.5%, ARM +4%), a big earnings surprise at NFLX +14%, and optimism for President Trump’s pro-business policies. Not to be left behind, the DOW is rising after PG beat earnings expectations. Overseas, the STOXX 50 has returned to levels not seen since 2000 as it rides the global AI-driven tech rally.     

 

Stargate AI Project: After the close, President Trump announced a public/private joint venture with Open AI, Softbank (SFTBY), and Oracle (ORCL) to build AI infrastructure in the US. The companies plan to put $100B into the initiative and raise the investment to $500B over four years. We expect additional AI plays to move on the news, e.g., the data center component companies Celestica (CLS), Ciena (CIEN), and VRT Holdings (VRT), etc., and data center contractor Hochtief (HOT +4%).

 

Market Musings: “The US economy is shifting from the most anti-business administration in history to the most business-friendly administration. In terms of the markets, it’s complicated. We have an earnings yield to bond yield ratio that’s probably the most unattractive level in 30 years.” (Billionaire fund manager Stanley Druckenmiller)

 

Speaking at the World Economic Forum, JP Morgan CEO Jamie Dimon said, “Asset prices are kind of inflated, by any measure. They are in the top 10% or 15% of historical valuations.” Dimon cited global deficit spending, inflation, and geopolitical tensions as threats to the bull market.

 

Renewable Energy: Wind energy and renewable companies are under pressure after President Trump signed an executive order on Monday halting US offshore wind lease sales and halted the issuance of approvals, permits, and loans for both onshore and offshore wind projects. We note that the US is the number two global producer of wind electricity generation at 21% of the total (75% is in Texas). Trump also eliminated Investment Tax Credits (ITCs) previously made available by the oxymoronic “Inflation Reduction Act” (Green New Deal). Renewable Energy companies at risk include GE Vernova (GEV), NextEra Energy (NEE), Constellation Energy (CEG),  Vestas Wind Systems (VWSYF), Brookfield Renewable (BEPC), Algonquin Power (AQN), Renewable Energy Systems (RES) and Daqo New Energy (DQ).
