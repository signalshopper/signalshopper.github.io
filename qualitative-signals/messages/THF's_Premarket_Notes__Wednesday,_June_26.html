Are we in a recession?

The Consumer Confidence Index (CCI) has two components: The Present Situation Index (40%) and the Expectations Index (60%). The Expectations Index reflects the six-month outlook for business, employment, and income (a gauge of future trends). A reading below 80 "typically" signals a recession.

 

As of yesterday, the Expectations Index (EI) has been below 80 for the fifth consecutive month. In June, the EI declined to 73.0 from 74.9 in May, and the June Consumer Confidence Index declined to 100.4 from a downwardly revised 101.3 in May and 114 in January. Are we in a recession?
