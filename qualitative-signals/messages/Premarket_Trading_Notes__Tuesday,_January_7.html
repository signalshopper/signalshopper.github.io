Treasuries:  3M 4.30 ( -- )  2YR 4.28 ( -- )  5YR 4.43 (-1 bp)  10YR 4.63 (-1 bp)  30YR 4.86 ( -- )

  • * The 10YR remains close to 4.6%, an eight-month high, as traders await this week’s employment reports.
  • * Yields on long-term bonds are rising faster than yields on short-term bonds. The widening spread between the short and long-term bonds – a steepening yield curve - implies that investors expect the Fed to lower the Fed Funds rate, which weakens the USD and causes foreign banks to stop buying longer-term US Treasuries. As demand falls, yields rise to attract buyers.
  • * Remember, short-term rates are more tied to the Fed Funds rate - because the Fed can speed up or slow down the economy with short-term rates. For this reason, we are adding 3M rates.
