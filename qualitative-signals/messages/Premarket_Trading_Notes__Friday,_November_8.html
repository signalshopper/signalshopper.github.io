Premarket Futures:  DJIA +0.08%  S&P -0.04%  NASDAQ -0.22%  10YR -2 bps  Gold -0.04%  Oil -1.48%  BTC +1.4%

 

Markets look to open slightly lower, but excluding the 10:00 ET release of preliminary Consumer Confidence, falling 10YR and 30YR treasury yields, and a declining VIX suggest markets will end the week in positive territory.  

 

Watching: There’s a lot of talk about inflation, but we remain concerned about declining demand for gasoline, a good signal of economic health. Last week, US gasoline demand declined to 8.2M barrels/day from 9.15M barrels/day. Rising inflation and declining demand can lead to stagflation. We hope and expect the “garbage man” will turn the tide.

 

Dow Theory: According to the DOW Theory, the Dow Industrials and Dow Transports must trend higher in tandem to confirm a bull market. In simple terms, transports take what industrials make. On Wednesday, both sectors put up record prints (Dow Transports +5.4% to 890.44, Dow industrials +3.6% to 1,508.05). Is this an overbought or a buy signal? We don’t know, but it’s a positive sign of market breadth. What’s driving the bullish sentiment? The pro-growth agenda of the Trump Administration (tax cuts and deregulation).
