The market looks to open slightly higher after weaker-than-expected employment numbers and ahead of tomorrow’s inflation data. Weighing on the markets are the US wholesale inventories. They were up 0.6% month/month to $901.6B in May, the largest increase since November 2022 and much higher than forecasts of 0.1% (stagflation?). Also weighing on the market is a decline in 1Q corporate profits and a weak 1Q GDP print.
