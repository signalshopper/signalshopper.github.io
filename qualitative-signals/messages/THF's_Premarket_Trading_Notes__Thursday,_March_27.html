US stock futures remain undecided as traders balance last night’s tariff bluster against somewhat positive economic data (see below). The Trump administration announced a 25% tariff on “all cars not made in the US,” set to take effect on April 2, while Trump warned of imposing larger tariffs on the EU and Canada if they coordinate efforts to counter US trade measures. Markets fear the tariffs will hurt the US economy and drive inflation higher. 

 

We will discuss this tomorrow, but we believe the problem is excess money stimulation sloshing around, driving valuations higher. The US is the largest economy in the world, and unspent funds on overseas products will find substitute products (e.g., autos) in the US. As always, we remind investors that we have been wrong before.
