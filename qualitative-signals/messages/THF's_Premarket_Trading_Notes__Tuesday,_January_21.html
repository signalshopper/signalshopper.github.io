We should expect global volatility today as traders assess the impact of President Trump’s executive orders. At first glance, markets look to open higher after the President said he would sign a memorandum to study trade policies, stopping short of imposing new [inflationary] levies. Treasuries and the USD declined on the news (positive for risk assets), energy prices are lower on “drill baby, drill” supply concerns, and digital assets are lower (BTC had rallied to $109k) after the President failed to mention crypto during his inauguration speeches. Today’s lack of economic reports may shift the focus back to earnings.   

 

Valuation/Bond Vigilantes Part 4: As of Friday, the S&P 500’s forward 12-month PE is 21.6. The 5-year average is 19.7, and the 10-year average is 18.2. On Tuesday, the 10-year treasury fell to 4.63% from a 14-month high of 4.8%, finishing the week at 4.61% (after December’s CPI report). Recall that declining yields make stocks more appealing relative to bonds.  

 

Will rates continue to rise? The hyperbolic tariff chatter remains unknown (is it saber rattling or actual policy?). Still, ballooning deficits remain a constant, and on Friday, the new administration informed Congress that aid to CA would be tethered to raising the debt ceiling (inflationary). The offset to higher deficits would be a booming Trump economy.

 

A final thought. There is hope. The S&P 500’s forward PE rose to a record 24x during the dot com bubble, with some tech startups trading as high as 200x before the market collapsed.

 

The Trump Train: “A businessman’s job isn’t to change the world but to cope with it,” said Wilbur Ross, a billionaire and Trump 47’s former Commerce Secretary, as World Economic Forum attendees suddenly become Trump bulls, citing his pro-business agenda. (Bloomberg)
