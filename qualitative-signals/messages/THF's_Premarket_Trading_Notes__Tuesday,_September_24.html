Premarket Futures:  DOW +0.17%  S&P +0.10%  NASDAQ +0.17%  10YR +6 bps  Gold +1.75  Crude +1.75  BTC +0.40%

 

Markets continue to drift higher ahead of hawkish Fed Governor Michelle Bowman’s 9:00ET comments this morning. Last week, Bowman voted for a 25 basis point rate reduction on signs of persistent price pressures. Wednesday, we will hear from eight Federal Reserve members, and on Friday, traders will look closely at the PCE Index for clues on the direction and size of future rate reductions.   

 

Yesterday, markets closed slightly higher after Fed officials Raphael Bostic, Neel Kashkari, and Austan Goolsbee expressed Support for the recent rate cut and indicated a preference for additional rate reductions in the upcoming months “if economic conditions remain as they are.”

 

The SEC has approved BlackRock Bitcoin ETF (IBIT) options, creating hopes of a gamma squeeze. The next step is approval by the less stringent Options Clearing Corporation (OCC) and the Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC). Approval by the OCC and CFTC  should attract liquidity and increase demand. Note: A gamma squeeze occurs when stock prices rise rapidly because market makers buy more stock to cover their derivative positions, driven by demand for call options.

 

Is Bad news good news for Europe? Flash PMI data for Europe signaled a sharper-than-expected slowdown in manufacturing and services, improving expectations for a rate reduction (currently 3.65%).
