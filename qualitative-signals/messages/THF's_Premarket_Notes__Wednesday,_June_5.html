Markets

 

Markets look to open higher after the ADP private sector jobs report came in lower-than-expected (+152K versus expectations of +175K). After market participants combined Monday’s weak ISM Manufacturing PMI and Tuesday’s below forecast JOLTS report with the ADP report, expectations for a September rate cut rose to 67%    

 

Premarket Futures:  DOW +0.28  S&P +0.37%  NASDAQ +0.65%  Gold +0.63%  Crude Oil +0.82%  BTC +3.0%

 

Unrealized Bank Losses: A new report from the FDIC revealed “Unrealized losses on available-for-sale and held-to-maturity securities increased by $39B to $517B in the 1Q. Higher unrealized losses on residential mortgage-backed securities, resulting from higher mortgage rates in the first quarter, drove the overall increase. This is the ninth quarter of unusually high unrealized losses since the Federal Reserve began raising interest rates in 1Q 2022.” (FDIC)

 

The FDIC also said that the number of lenders on its Problem Bank List rose to 63 in the 1Q of 2024 from 52 in the 4Q of 2023. These are banks on the brink of insolvency due to financial, operational, or managerial weakness or a combination of such issues. We need not worry [yet] as these problem banks only represent 1.4% of total banks, but we need to be aware of the growing problem as higher for longer rates are likely to push more banks onto the problem bank list.
