Risk is back on after a weaker-than-expected employment report (142K jobs added versus expectations of 160K). The 10-year yield declined to 3.69 (-4 bps), the lowest in nearly 16 months, as evidence of a softening labor market strengthened bets of multiple Fed Funds rate cuts. We expect the market to rally at the open (on expectations for aggressive rate cuts) and then pull back on the reality of a recessionary environment. We will learn more when bearish Fed President Williams and bearish Fed Governor Waller speak at 8:45 and 11:00, respectively.
