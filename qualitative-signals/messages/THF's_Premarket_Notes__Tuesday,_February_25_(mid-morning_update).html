Futures are fluctuating around the flatline after the S&P 500 and NASDAQ extended their losing streak to three sessions on Monday. Geopolitical and trade tensions continue to weigh on sentiment after President Trump confirmed that tariffs on Mexican and Canadian imports will take effect next week, and reports surfaced that the US is tightening restrictions on China’s chip industry and pressuring other governments to do the same. Semiconductor stocks are under pressure, with Nvidia down 0.8% and Intel slipping 0.7% in premarket trading, while Qualcomm and Broadcom hovered near the flatline.

 

The market may tread water ahead of the 10:00 ET Consumer Confidence number (102.4 expected).

 

Inflation: The Congressional Budget Office projects that inflation, measured by Personal Consumption Expenditures (PCE), will fall to 2.2% in 2025, down from 2.5% last year, with inflation reaching the Federal Reserve’s goal of 2% by 2027. (Congressional Budget Office.) The Conference Board, an economic think tank, predicts inflation will fade to 2% by the end of the year. We note that the last administration left us with a price problem. Inflation may come down (our view), but prices are sticky and unlikely to fall unless the economy implodes and deflation follows.  

 

PCE: The Fed’s preferred inflation number will be released Friday. The CPI and PPI inflation numbers have come in hotter than expected. Recently, the market has withstood the news, writing it off to seasonal effects. Friday, expectations are for the core number to decrease from 2.8% to 2.64%. An in-line or better number would be a positive catalyst for a troubled market.
