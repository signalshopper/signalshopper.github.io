"The retail industry is going through a tough time as it copes with inflation-weary consumers and a rash of bankruptcies, prompting chains to announce the closures of almost 3,200 brick-and-mortar stores so far in 2024. That's a 24% increase from a year ago, according to CoreSight, which tracks store closures and openings across the US."

 

As we noted yesterday, the July retail sales surprise was driven primarily by autos and auto parts sales, which rebounded after last month's computer glitch delayed sales. Excluding the bump in auto sales, retail sales rose just 0.4% last month (not 1.0%), which is only slightly ahead of the 0.3% forecast. "Now you know the rest of the [retail] story." Paul Harvey
