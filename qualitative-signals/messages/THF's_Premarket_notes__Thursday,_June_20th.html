A "recession is underway," and we're just waiting for the National Bureau of Economic Research's corroboration (which typically lags by 1-year). Jack Schannep, former editor of TheDowTheory.com, bases his conviction on an indicator he created in 2000 called the "Schannep Recession Indicator" (SRI), which corresponds to trends in the US unemployment rate.

 

The SRI is based on the 3-month moving average of the unemployment rate. A recession signal is triggered when it rises by at least 0.4% from that moving average's prior cyclical low. The SRI's latest signal was triggered on June 7 with the report of May's unemployment rate of 4.0%, bringing the 3-month moving average to 3.9%, or 0.4% higher than the moving average in April of last year. https://www.marketwatch.com/story/the-latest-unemployment-report-has-triggered-this-spot-on-recession-indicator-08f2785d?mod=mw_more_headlines
