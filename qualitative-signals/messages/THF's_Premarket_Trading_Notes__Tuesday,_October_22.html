Stocks look to open lower as traders reduce bets for a 50 bps rate cut and increase expectations for a deficit-spending DJ Trump win. We note today’s lack of economic news and the massive number of companies reporting earnings this week (~20% of the S&P).   

 

Can we talk about the Treasury market? Expectations for aggressive rate cuts continue to fade as economic reports signal a stronger-than-expected (real or imagined) economy. Combining the Federal Reserve’s reluctance to cut rates with the lack of interest by either political party to balance the budget, you may understand why Treasury yields continue to rise (yesterday, the 10YR rose 11 bps/+0.11% to 1.19%).

 

In addition, the Fed has been shrinking its balance sheet (reducing supply by reducing bank reserves). The money available to lend has declined to $7 trillion from $9T at its 2022 peak as ~ $25B in Treasuries and $35B in mortgage-backed securities continue to roll off each month without reinvesting.

 

If Fed Funds rates remain higher than expected, currently 4.75% to 5.0%, we should expect small-caps (Russell 2000) to continue underperforming.
