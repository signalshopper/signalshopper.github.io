Stock futures look to open lower after the large-cap banks print good quarterly earnings results but, as expected, warn of economic turbulence ahead (JPM -2.09%, WFC -4.85%, etc.). Today, we also had BlackRock’s Larry Fink dog piling on the rabbit when he said today’s economic environment reminds him of the 2008 financial crisis.

 

Volatility Drivers: Tariff uncertainty, recession fears, valuations, sticky inflation, and geopolitical risks continue to stoke volatility.

 

The relationship between stocks and bonds, i.e., stocks go up when bonds go down, and vice versa, appears broken. Questions for investors include: Will tariffs raise inflation, will foreigners dump US bonds, and are hedge funds unwinding their basis trades? We’re not certain, but we believe it’s the latter because, in uncertain times, the cheap money hedge funds need to make the arbitrage trade becomes hard to find.
