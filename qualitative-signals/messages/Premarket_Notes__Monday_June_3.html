Premarket Futures:  DOW  -0.04%  S&P +0.21%  NASDAQ +0.52%  10YR -3 bps  BTC +2.7%  Oil -0.36%

 

Markets are rallying in anticipation of interest rate cuts by the European Central Bank and the Bank of Canada later this week, as well as last Friday’s neutral PCE report. Treasury yields and the USD are lower (positive risk assets). BTC is up along with other risk assets.  

 

However, the Federal Reserve remains concerned about sticky inflation and many of the Fed’s talking heads said as much last week. Their comments suggest Friday’s May Employment Report, the last significant data point before the Fed meets on June 11-12, will be meaningful as a strong job market will signal higher for longer rates. We remain concerned about the potential for economic stagflation and the almost singular focus on the technology sector, particularly any company that mentions AI in its quarterly report.

 

We note that the S&P 500 is trading at an expensive 20.6X earnings of $243.30, and 1Q revenues were about one-half of last year’s 1Q revenues. But until the $21T of M2 money supply stops washing around the economy, looking for a home, the game continues.
