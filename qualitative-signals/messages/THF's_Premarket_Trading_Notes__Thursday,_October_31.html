A Global Trade War? Trump has called for up to 60% tariffs on Chinese imports. Is this a good or bad idea? Decades ago, US leaders assumed that bringing China into the IMF and the World Trade Organization would make the country more market-oriented. But that didn’t happen. In many ways, it made it worse as China moved to boost manufacturing and exports, while holding down consumption within the country, resulting in a massive trade surplus which negatively impacts its trade partners worldwide.

 

In the US, China’s growth wiped out US factory jobs in what came to be known as The China Shock (the years between 1990 - 2010s when the US saw job losses, income losses, and labor market variation) and the growing flood of cheap manufacturing imports is being called China Shock 2.0. The difference this time around is that the world is fighting back - the US and Europe are raising trade barriers to Chinese-made EVs and renewable energy gear, and Brazil, India, Indonesia, and Mexico are zeroing in on Chinese imports of steel, ceramics, and chemicals dumped on their domestic markets. Yes, a global trade war is brewing. (Paraphrased from the The Bitcoin Layer)
