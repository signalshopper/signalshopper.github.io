Markets look to open lower as the fear gauge (VIX) rises and the 10YR remains elevated. We note that yields above 4.5% can precipitate a stock market correction. Today, we may sit on the sidelines as year-end tax selling, profit-taking, and inflation fears distort objective signals.  

 

Valuation: Wells Fargo’s global strategist “sees the S&P 500 somewhere between 6,400 and 6,800 by the end of 2025, a gain of 7% to 14% from Frisay’s close.” The average forecast is 9.1% on earnings growth of 14.7%. However, the small-caps (Russell 2000) continue to lag, trading at their 50-day moving average (DMA). A move back above its 20 DMA is needed for the market to become more bullish on the index, but enthusiasm for small-caps is likely to remain low until the Federal Reserve becomes less hawkish. Where’s the rub? Despite continuing unemployment claims rising to a three-year high, many analysts lowered their expectations to one 25 bps rate cut this year from two to three as the economy, inflated by the Administration’s generosity, continues to grow above expectations.

 

Jimmy Carter: Our condolences go out to Jimmy Carter’s family and friends, and congratulations to the former president for living long enough to learn that he will no longer be remembered as the worst president in modern history.
