Markets

 

Markets look to open higher after initial unemployment claims fell more than expected on the week ending Aug 3. This report alleviates some concerns a softening labor market precludes a US recession. This reduces expectations for a recession and provides air to the theory the labor market is okay. We are not a believer and expect these numbers to be revised upwards at a later date.  

 

Buy the dip? “Quant funds that chase the hottest trades are getting thrashed as momentum bets backfired all at once.

Going into July, quants were positioned for the year’s big trades (e.g., AI trades, Mag-7, etc.) to keep gathering momentum and betting against developed-nation government bonds (the yen carry trade).” Without this exuberant demand, it’s hard to see how markets trade higher in the short term.

 

 

Recession Watch: JPM’s CEO, Jamie Dimon, said “he’s skeptical inflation will return to the Federal Reserve’s 2% target, citing risks including deficit spending and “remilitarization of the world.” (Bloomberg) In other words, inflation may remain elevated for the foreseeable future, and Mr. Dimon believes there’s not much the Fed can do about it if saving jobs is its goal.
