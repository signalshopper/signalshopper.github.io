Markets look to open down after Core PCE, the Fed’s preferred measure of inflation, was reported higher than expected. “The headline PCE price index grew 2.5% on an annual basis during the month, unchanged from Jan’s reading, but the “core” metric, which strips out more volatile items like food and fuel, came in at 2.8% annually, slightly above January’s revised higher 2.7%.” We believe the economic numbers are not that bad (see Economic Reports below).

 

Valuation: The stock market’s higher moves seem to be nothing more than oversold rallies. If the SPX (5693) makes new lows (below 5500 and 5400), investors should consider the possibility of a longer-lasting bear market. Buy the Apr 4 555 Puts?

 

The problem, in our view, is that the Federal Reserve’s monetary policy (versus a balance your budget policy) leaves too much excess cash in the market. Last Friday, Governor Christopher Waller confirmed our view when he said, “Slowing further or stopping redemptions of securities holdings will be appropriate as we get closer to an ample level of reserves. But in my view, we are not there yet because reserve balances stand at over $3 T, and this level is abundant.” 

 

Are AI bubble valuations coming to an end? After yesterday’s close, the highly anticipated CoreWeave IPO (CRWV) priced at $40/share (a $19B valuation), versus expectations of $47-$55 ($27B-$32B), as the AI bubble and market sentiment dampened demand. CoreWeave develops data centers designed to run AI applications. It currently operates 32 data centers across the US and Europe. The read-through to AI stocks could be negative today.

 

Is copper the new gold? Copper prices remain elevated amid stress on domestic smelters due to President Trump’s threat of tariffs/sanctions and the demand driven by the $0.55 per pound arbitrage between the New York (COMEX) and London (LME) exchanges. Notably, the US imports ~45% of its copper. If the President’s copper tariffs become a reality, dependency on domestic capacity, which is limited to only two major smelters, Southern Copper (SCCO, $82B) and Freeport-McMoran (FCX, $68B), would intensify. ETF options include The US Copper Index (CPER, $31.96, +30% YTD) and USCF 2X Copper Index (CPXR, $27.09, 45% YTD). Risks: Copper prices are volatile and influenced by global supply and demand (construction, manufacturing, automotive, cleantech/renewable energy), geopolitical events, and economic conditions, i.e., inflation (pos)/deflation (neg).
